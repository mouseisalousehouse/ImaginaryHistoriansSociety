Submitted theses will be reviewed by an admissions committee of three.
Each committee member shall make an evaluation
which will be one of three possibilities:

- ACCEPT
- REJECT
- REQUEST REVISION

If all three members of the committee ACCEPT the thesis, the character
is a member of the Imaginary Historians Society as a PhD Student, and
granted all the rights and privileges thereof.

If any one of the committee members REJECT a thesis, the thesis is not
accepted and the character must submit another one.

If one or more committee members REQUEST REVISION of a thesis, supplying
suggestions and notes, the character may revise and resubmit. The revised
thesis will then be evaluated by each committee member with either
ACCEPT AFTER REVISION or REJECT.

Article V
==========

The Director is an office appointed by the council whenever a vacancy occurs, by resignation, death, or removal from office. A unanimous vote among all three Councilmembers is needed to appoint or
remove a Director.
The Director oversees the day-to-day operations of the Society.

Article VI
==========

The Bylaws of The Imaginary Historians’ Society can be amended by either two votes from the Council and the approval of the Director or three votes from the Council.

Article VII
==========

The Imaginary Historians’ Society shall maintain and operate an Archive of Important Evidences, which is to preserve papers, objects, and all material which is deemed permanently valuable as an aid to understanding the imaginary history, or is of interest to the Society.

Article VIII
==========

The Imaginary Historians’ Society shall operate an Imaginary Historians’ Society Press to publish the output of its members.

Article IX
==========

The Imaginary Historians’ Society shall operate a library to aid all who wish in research.
